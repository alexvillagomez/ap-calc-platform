# MCAT Depth-Reference Research: Authoritative Sources for Per-Keyword Facts

**Research Date:** 2026-06-30  
**Scope:** Identifying commercially-licensable, reasoning-depth (L2–L4) reference corpus for grounding ~2,769 per-keyword "must-state facts" lists in the Lodera MCAT taxonomy.

---

## Executive Summary

**LICENSING DECISIVE FINDING:** Khan Academy (CC BY-NC-SA) and OpenStax (mostly CC BY-NC-SA) are **legally disqualified for commercial products** due to NonCommercial clauses. The AAMC official guide is proprietary (licensing unclear; requires legal review). UWorld is copyright-proprietary (non-ingestible). **Only viable primary source: OpenStax Biology for AP® Courses (CC BY — commercial-allowed exception) + supplementary depth from authoritative secondary references (MilesDown calibration, AAMC learning objectives, Kaplan/Princeton Review synthesis).**

## 1. AAMC–Khan Academy MCAT Collection

### Availability
- **Status:** STILL ACTIVE (not sunset). Khan Academy will host MCAT content until 2026 (per AAMC partnership).
- **Content:** 1,100 videos + 3,000 review questions covering all four exam sections.
- **Link:** [Khan Academy MCAT Prep](https://www.khanacademy.org/test-prep/mcat)

### License
- **Type:** CC BY-NC-SA (Creative Commons Attribution–NonCommercial–ShareAlike)
- **Citation:** "May be reproduced and distributed with attribution for **educational and noncommercial purposes only**."
- **Verdict:** ❌ **DISQUALIFIED FOR COMMERCIAL PRODUCT.** The NonCommercial clause explicitly bars use in a paid study platform.

### Depth Coverage (L2–L4)
- **Reasoning Depth:** Yes — videos emphasize conceptual reasoning + worked examples, not bare flashcard facts.
- **Issue:** Khan Academy's scope is **introductory college-level** (general biology, chemistry, physics) — it provides L2–L3 conceptual depth but lacks the L4 mechanistic detail (e.g., specific enzyme kinetics, amino acid properties, precise bioenergetic values) needed for MCAT edge-case reasoning.

### Bottom Line
Authoritative but legally disqualified; depth insufficient for advanced reasoning grounding.

## 2. AAMC Official Content Outline & Learning Objectives

### Availability & Format
- **Source:** [AAMC "What's on the MCAT Exam" PDF Outline](https://students-residents.aamc.org/prepare-mcat-exam/whats-mcat-exam-pdf-outline)
- **Scope:** Organized by foundational concepts, content categories, and Scientific Inquiry & Reasoning Skills (SIRS).
- **Content:** Lists categories such as "Structure and Function of Proteins and Their Constituent Amino Acids" and "Transmission of Genetic Information."

### Enumeration Depth
- **Level of Detail:** Stays **relatively vague** — outlines categories and broad competencies, not granular testable facts.
- **Example:** Does NOT specify "amino acid pKa values" or "exact glycolysis ATP yield" (those are implicit, not enumerated).
- **Assessment:** Provides **architecture** (umbrella keywords, foundational concepts) but not atomic facts or L3–L4 mechanistic specifics.

### License & Reusability
- **Status:** AAMC proprietary — licensing unclear from public sources.
- **Note:** AAMC is testing authority (not academic publisher), so licensing assumptions may differ from educational content providers.
- **Verdict:** ⚠️ **UNCERTAIN — legal review required** before ingestion into a paid product.

### Bottom Line
Authoritative but limited enumeration depth; licensing ambiguous; suitable only as architectural framing, not as a facts corpus.

### Verdict

## 5. Commercial Proprietary Sources (Depth Gold Standards)

### UWorld MCAT QBank
- **Status:** Widely regarded as **gold standard for MCAT depth** (parallel to UWorld's USMLE dominance).
- **Depth:** Comprehensive L3–L4 mechanistic explanations (entire answer rationales for correct + incorrect choices).
- **Reasoning Quality:** Deep, integrative reasoning — aligns with MCAT's "mechanism-based" emphasis.
- **License:** ❌ **Proprietary copyright** — non-ingestible; no machine-readable corpus release.
- **Use Case:** Human calibration + exemplar reasoning depth standard (not primary reference corpus).
- **Source:** [UWorld MCAT](https://gradschool.uworld.com/mcat/)

### Kaplan MCAT Complete 7-Book Subject Review
- **Status:** Major commercial test-prep standard; proprietary "Medflix" videos + 14 full-length practice tests.
- **Depth:** L2–L3 conceptual, L3 mechanistic (less deep than UWorld, more structured than Khan).
- **License:** ❌ **Proprietary copyright** — non-ingestible.
- **Use Case:** Calibration for pedagogical clarity, not primary facts source.

### Princeton Review MCAT Course
- **Status:** Comparable to Kaplan; 16 full-length practice tests, proprietary "Medflix" videos.
- **Depth:** L2–L3, balanced simplicity-vs-detail.
- **License:** ❌ **Proprietary copyright** — non-ingestible.
- **Use Case:** Calibration standard, human depth reference.

### MilesDown & AnKing MCAT Decks
- **Role:** Community-maintained flashcard repositories (Anki format); widely used as depth calibration.
- **Depth Standard:** L1–L2 recognition facts (memorizable atomic units), not L3–L4 mechanistic integration.
- **License:** ❌ **User-generated; copyright status unclear** — not suitable as authoritative reference corpus.
- **Use Case:** Calibration for card-level granularity, not authoritative depth source.

## 6. Final Recommendation: Primary + Layered Approach

### Tier 1: Primary Depth-Reference Corpus

**OpenStax Biology for AP® Courses (CC BY)**
- **Why:** Only open-source, legally-clear (CC BY) resource with peer-reviewed depth and MCAT relevance.
- **Licensing:** ✅ CC BY allows commercial use with attribution.
- **Scope:** Comprehensive biology + biochemistry foundation (L2–L3).
- **Coverage:** ~70–80% of in_depth keywords need foundation facts grounded here.
- **Mapping Plan:** 
  1. Parse OpenStax Biology AP text into atomic concept units (section-by-section).
  2. Cosine-embed each unit via text-embedding-3-small.
  3. For each of ~1,500 biology/biochemistry keywords, retrieve top-5 units; curate 3–5 "must-state facts" per keyword.
  4. Fallback: keywords with no OpenStax alignment → manual research (below).

### Tier 2: Supplementary Depth (for L3–L4 gaps)

**AAMC Official Content Outline + Learning Objectives** (as architectural scaffold)
- **Why:** Authoritative, free AAMC guidance on scope.
- **Use:** Identify categories + learning objectives; use as a checklist to ensure coverage completeness.
- **Caveat:** ⚠️ Licensing uncertain — cite as "AAMC scope framework" in disclaimers, do NOT ingest text directly.

**UWorld MCAT (human calibration, not ingestion)**
- **Why:** Gold-standard depth reasoning model — read ~200 high-complexity questions to internalize L3–L4 reasoning patterns.
- **Use:** Inform prompt engineering for AI fact-enumeration; validate generated facts against UWorld's mechanistic depth.
- **Not Ingested:** Copyright proprietary.

**Kaplan / Princeton Review (selected passages for specific mechanisms)**
- **Why:** Clearer pedagogical treatment of some topics (e.g., enzyme kinetics) than OpenStax.
- **Use:** Limited, selective excerpts for non-verbatim calibration (quote short explanations as pedagogical examples, not facts corpus).
- **Licensing:** ⚠️ Proprietary — use sparingly; fair use may cover brief pedagogical citations.

### Tier 3: Fill Gaps

**Manual research + subject-matter expert review**
- **Scope:** ~20–30% of keywords (edge cases, advanced mechanisms) not covered by OpenStax.
- **Process:** 
  1. Identify uncovered keywords (those with poor OpenStax embeddings).
  2. Research via authoritative secondary sources (biochemistry textbooks, AAMC released exams with explanations, peer-reviewed journal abstracts for mechanism papers).
  3. Subject-matter expert (e.g., MCAT tutor, medical student) review for depth + accuracy.

## Mapping Plan: OpenStax → ~2,769 Per-Keyword Facts

### Step 1: Prepare OpenStax Corpus
```bash
# Chunk OpenStax Biology for AP® Courses into ~2,000–5,000 semantic units
# (chapters/sections + subsections; ~500–1,500 words per unit for dense facts)
# Embed each unit via text-embedding-3-small
# Store in vector DB with chapter/section metadata
```

### Step 2: For Each Keyword
```
Input: keyword (name, description, scope_contract)
1. Embed keyword name + scope_contract
2. Cosine search vector DB for top-10 OpenStax units (similarity ≥ 0.75)
3. LLM extraction prompt:
   - "Given these OpenStax passages, extract 3–5 atomic 'must-state facts' 
      that a student must know to solve MCAT questions on [keyword]."
   - Validate that facts:
     - Are L2–L3 mechanistic (not vague, not bare recognition)
     - Are testable (can be asked directly or inferred from a passage)
     - Are complete (no dangling references to unrelated keywords)
4. Store facts + metadata (source unit, embedding similarity, confidence)
```

### Step 3: Gap Audit + Manual Fill
```
1. Identify keywords with <0.70 average embedding similarity (weak OpenStax coverage)
2. Manual research + UWorld calibration for each
3. Expert review pass (medical student / MCAT tutor signs off on depth)
```

### Step 4: Validation
```
1. Cross-check facts against AAMC learning objectives (completeness)
2. Benchmark ~100 random facts against UWorld QBank reasoning depth
3. A/B test: keywords grounded in OpenStax facts vs. ungrounded → measure AI-generated question quality
```

### Impact on Ingestion Pipeline

The `scripts/ingest-openstax.ts` pipeline enforces a hard license gate at runtime:
- Each book in `BOOK_REGISTRY` carries `blocked: true/false` based on the verified license.
- CC BY-NC-SA books exit with an error and instructions to obtain a Rice University reuse exception.
- The BOOK_REGISTRY can be updated to `blocked: false` once a written reuse agreement is in hand.

### Path to Unblocking

To ingest MCAT-relevant OpenStax titles (Biology 2e, Chemistry 2e, etc.):
1. Submit a reuse request: https://openstax.org/contact (or the OpenStax Content Reuse Request Form).
2. Wait for written approval from Rice University.
3. In `scripts/ingest-openstax.ts`, change the relevant `BOOK_REGISTRY` entry: `blocked: false`, `license: "CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 (reuse approved — [date])"`.
4. Run: `npm run openstax:ingest -- --book biology-2e --chapter 1 --dry-run` to verify, then drop `--dry-run`.

### Alternative: Reuse Under Fair Use or Educational Exception

Fair use may cover non-verbatim extraction for AI grounding (internal use, no redistribution of the OpenStax text). This is a legal gray area. If the product remains B2C with a paid tier, consult legal counsel before proceeding without a reuse exception.
